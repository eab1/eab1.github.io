Why has China invested heavily in Africa?

Project aims

This data science project aims to show, through data, some of the main motivations driving China's significant 21st century investment in Africa. Discussing China’s reasons for their African investment is important as the country is a major global player and its actions have significant economic and political implications for the continent and the rest of the world. I will be defining Chinese investment as the provision of loans and other direct investments in businesses and infrastructure projects.

Demographics and population factors

The contrast between China's ageing population, with an average age of 38, and Africa's youthful population, with an average age of 18, presents an opportunity for China to tap into the continent's cheap labour force and growing consumer market. This investment has helped to support China's economic growth and will continue to do so despite the country facing a declining active workforce.

Political influence

This graph explores the relationship between a government's perceived level of corruption and the total loan value it received from 2000-2020. The weak correlation between the two variables as indicated by a low R2 value suggest China does not favour investing in countries with a certain level of corruption but rather their FDI is spread across a wide range of corruption level rankings. China's investment strategy allows them to forge business relationships with a variety of countries, supporting their aim of increasing political influence in the continent. Conclusion

In conclusion, the motivations behind China's significant investment in Africa are multifaceted and can be understood by various push and pull factors. These include demographic changes in both regions, Africa's abundant natural resources, the growth of its middle class and cities, and the formation of political alliances.
